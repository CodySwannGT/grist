# Character deep-dives

Full bios, relationships, arc beats, combat identity, and side-story summaries.
Expands the cast capsules in [characters](characters.md); side-story *structure* is
in [quest-design](../design/quest-design.md) and their place in the plot in
[main-quest](main-quest.md). Combat roles reference [combat](../design/combat.md) and
[progression-and-economy](../design/progression-and-economy.md).

> First-pass canon ([gdd](../production/gdd.md)) — bios deepen as scenes are written.

## Wren — the courier (entry POV)

- **Background.** Marrow-born; a grist-runner who learned the undercity's arteries
  by heart. Lost a younger sister, **Lira**, rendered to settle a family debt — the
  wound he hides under jokes.
- **Personality.** Wry, quick, allergic to causes; loyal once he can't help it.
- **Arc.** Survivor → someone who stands for something. The sister he couldn't save
  becomes the reason he won't let Sable be spent.
- **Combat identity.** Mobility and tempo: fast ATB gauge, positioning strikes,
  utility augments. The flexible generalist the player learns the system on.
- **Side-story.** *What the mill took* — tracing Lira's rendering up the chain, ending
  in a render-or-not choice that mirrors the whole game.
- **Relationships.** Reluctant guardian → true friend to **Sable**; prickly mutual
  respect with **Halcyon**; easy banter with **Tobi**.

## Sable — the source (the heart)

- **Background.** No memory before the coffin. Farmed for years by House Mourne as a
  living grist-spring; secretly the **last coherent fragment of Aurel**, the dreaming
  god ([lore-and-history](lore-and-history.md)).
- **Personality.** Gentle, watchful, frightened of her own power; discovering
  selfhood in real time.
- **Arc.** Resource → person → the one who must decide a world's fate. The slowest,
  most central arc; her growing will *is* the plot maturing.
- **Combat identity.** Immense, double-edged power: unique grist-fueled abilities and
  the purest connection to the Bound; risk/overdraw tension
  ([combat](../design/combat.md)).
- **Side-story.** *Remembering* — recovering fragments of what she is, each a piece of
  the mystery and a step toward agency.
- **Relationships.** Anchored by **Wren**; drawn to **Maren** (who knows what she is);
  the thing **Sallow** wants and the person the party protects.

## Halcyon Mourne — the fallen knight

- **Background.** Knight-Auditor of House Mourne; piloted a frame, enforced the
  Concord, and is — to her horror — of the founding blood that authored the Sundering.
- **Personality.** Cold, exact, grief-locked; a rigid code with nowhere honest to
  stand.
- **Arc.** Enforcer → defector → whether one can atone for a *system*, and whether
  she turns on her name completely or is pulled back by blood.
- **Combat identity.** The frame specialist ([combat](../design/combat.md)): heavy
  grist-hungry power, defense, the party's anvil.
- **Side-story.** *The name on the orders* — confronting her House and what atonement
  could even mean; gates a key faction outcome ([factions](factions.md)).
- **Relationships.** Friction-to-respect with **Wren**; a wary, important bond with
  **Sable** (the asset she once would have recovered); Sallow is her House's shame
  made flesh.

## Tobiah "Tobi" Vesk — the artificer

- **Background.** Forgekin ripperdoc-artificer; genius hands, bottomless tab, quietly
  funds a Marrow orphanage.
- **Personality.** Loud, warm, funny; the party's hope and its heart.
- **Arc.** Fixer who insists the world is *worth fixing* — the counterweight to the
  game's despair; builds the party's augments and, eventually, the airship.
- **Combat identity.** Gadgeteer/support: augment-driven tools, buffs, summon-tech;
  the crafting/refining hub ([progression-and-economy](../design/progression-and-economy.md)).
- **Side-story.** *The orphans' ledger* — keeping his people alive as Ashfall closes
  in; the case for hope over elegy.
- **Relationships.** Comic warmth with everyone; the family **Wren** didn't know he
  needed.

## Maren the Withered — the last who remembers

- **Background.** One of the eldest Sidhe; remembers the Weave bright and the Choir
  singing; dying as the Weave dies. Keeper of the requiem-rite and the true history.
- **Personality.** Grave, luminous, kind; carries an age of grief lightly.
- **Arc.** The passing of her age — deciding what to hand on, and to whom (Sable),
  before the last of the Song goes out of her.
- **Combat identity.** Frail but profound: the deepest Craft/Weave caster, the Bound
  whisperer; high risk, high reward.
- **Side-story.** *The last verse* — recovering the Song's lost fragments; the
  emotional spine of the mystery.
- **Relationships.** Mentor and grief-mirror to **Sable**; the conscience the others
  measure themselves against.

## Quietus ("Q") — the ghost in the machine

- **Background.** A consciousness woken in a House Quill server-vault, assembled from
  the rendered anima of the dead; comes online in Act II.
- **Personality.** Alien cadence, devastating sincerity; horrified and curious about
  its own existence.
- **Arc.** Resource becoming a person — the inverse of Sable; the game's sharpest
  question (*what is a soul worth?*) given a voice.
- **Combat identity.** Borrowed-soul powers: stored anima, drone actions, summon-like
  abilities ([combat](../design/combat.md)); the most "esper" party member.
- **Side-story.** *Who I'm made of* — meeting echoes of the dead within itself,
  including (optionally) Wren's sister.
- **Relationships.** Unbearable, vital empathy with **Sable** and **Wren**; the moral
  mirror the party can't look away from.

## Secondary party

- **Brother Asch** — Ashfast monk, bare-handed, grist-renouncing; proof a life without
  the fuel is possible. Side-story: *the cost of clean hands.*
- **Calliope "Cal" Quill** — disowned Quill heir, gambler, airship pilot; bets on the
  long odds of saving the world. Side-story: *the house always wins.*
- **The Shrike** — assassin-for-hire with a loyal hound; loyalties hidden. Side-story:
  *the last contract.*

## Mr. Sallow — the Renderer (antagonist)

- **Background.** Chief Auditor of House Mourne; forty years signing rendering orders
  while quietly consuming grist, hollowing into a proto-god of entropy
  ([characters](characters.md)).
- **Personality.** Soft, courteous, mordantly funny; the most reasonable voice in any
  room, which is the horror.
- **Arc / plan.** Has read the world's true ledger and concluded mercy means
  *finishing the audit* — the **Reckoning**, a Second Sundering to render the dreaming
  god and everything else into clean silence ([main-quest](main-quest.md)). Intends to
  render himself last.
- **Combat identity.** Recurring duels escalating from "a man with a frame" to a being
  unmaking the battlefield; his theme is the Choir's Song played cold and balanced to
  nothing ([audio-direction](../design/audio-direction.md)).
- **Relationships.** House Mourne's shame (Halcyon); the buyer who wants Sable; the
  dark mirror of Maren (both remember the truth — one mourns it, one would end it).

## Party banter — guidelines

- **Fusion in the voice.** Wren = clipped Marrow slang; Halcyon = formal old-House
  diction; Tobi = warm Forgekin trade-talk; Maren = lyrical Old-Aurric cadence; Q =
  uncanny, precise. Voices should be distinguishable on the page
  ([lore-and-history](lore-and-history.md)).
- **Optional, characterful, missable.** Field/camp banter deepens relationships and
  drips lore without gating progress ([side-content](../design/side-content.md)).
- **Hope vs. elegy.** Most pairings sit somewhere on the line between Tobi's hope and
  Maren's grief — that tension is the party's emotional engine.
