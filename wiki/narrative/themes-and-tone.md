# Themes & tone

The fusion at the heart of GRIST is not aesthetic — it's thematic. Cyberpunk and
high fantasy are joined by a single idea, and everything else grows from it.

## The governing theme

**The commodification of the sacred — the disenchantment of the world.** GRIST
asks what happens when magic, gods, and souls become *resources*: priced, mined,
and burned. Cyberpunk supplies the machine that consumes; high fantasy supplies
the sacred thing being fed into it. The Lord of the Rings mourned the passing of
the age of magic as a natural sorrow; GRIST mourns it as a *murder for profit*.
The title says it: everything is grist for the mill.

## Supporting themes

- **Elegy — mourning a dying world.** The dominant emotional key. The world is
  ending slowly, and the game gives you time to grieve it. (See the Ashfall act in
  [story](story.md), and Maren in [characters](characters.md).)
- **Personhood — what counts as a soul when souls are fuel.** Carried by Sable
  (a person treated as a resource) and Quietus (a resource becoming a person).
- **Reform or refusal — can a system built on consumption be fixed, or only
  walked away from?** The Unrendered argue it out; the endings answer it.
- **Inheritance — what the dying owe the living, and the living the dead.** Maren's
  passing age, Halcyon's cursed name, Wren's lost sister, AUREL's heir.
- **The passing of wonders into the age of mortals.** The closing movement: maybe
  the best ending is the one where the gods are finally, mercifully gone.

## Tone

**Elegiac neon-noir over ancient stone.** Melancholy and mystery first, with real
warmth in the party and a vein of mordant, corporate-deadpan humor (most of it
Sallow's and Tobi's, from opposite ends of the moral spectrum). The slow burn is
tonal as much as structural: GRIST is patient, quiet, and unafraid of stillness.
It trusts the player to sit with grief and curiosity rather than be hurried by a
plot.

## Aesthetic & motifs

- **Chrome grafted onto rune; gold and ash; neon over old bone.** Every frame
  should read as both cyberpunk and fantasy at once — a circuit that is also a
  sigil, a server-vault that is also a reliquary.
- **The vertical world.** Wealth lives in the light at the top of Vanta; the truth
  lives in the dark at the bottom. Descent is the game's basic gesture — down the
  tiers, down to the Deep, down to the god's heart.
- **Rain, and the dimming of color.** A visual language for the dying Weave: the
  world is subtly, continuously losing its saturation as it runs down — a thing
  the art can dramatize and the Ashfall act can pay off.
- **The recurring fragment of song.** The Choir's lost music as a leitmotif —
  half-remembered by the Sidhe, surfacing at set-pieces (the requiem-rite),
  fully heard only at the end.

## Craft target

Presentation at least equal to the original **Final Fantasy VI**: expressive
16-bit pixel art and sprite animation, painterly backgrounds, and signature
musical set-pieces — elevated with modern lighting, parallax, and the
desaturation motif. The bar is FFVI's *grandeur*, not merely its resolution.

## Why it would be picked up

These themes are not decoration — they are the most current concerns in the
medium (AI and personhood, extraction economies, ecological/spiritual collapse,
the limits of reform) dressed in the genre's most beloved structure. GRIST is a
prestige JRPG with something to say, a singular look, and a name that is its
thesis. See [pitch](pitch.md).
