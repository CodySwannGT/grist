# Lore, history & timeline

The deep background of Aurd: the pantheon, the chronology, the languages, and how
people actually live. This is the canon writers keep consistent; it expands
[world](world.md) and underpins [main-quest](main-quest.md) and [story](story.md).

## Reckoning of years

Time is counted from the Sundering — **the Silencing of the Choir**. Years before
it are **BS** (Before Silence); after, **AS** (After Silence). The present of the
game is **≈ AS 920**.

## The Choir (the pantheon)

The old gods did not build the world; they **sang** it. Their song was the **Weave**
([world](world.md)). Five voices are remembered:

| Voice | Domain | Echo today |
|---|---|---|
| **Aurel**, the Dawnfather | Light; the World-Soul; the *keystone* of the song | Its corpse is Vanta's foundation; it still dreams |
| **Morra**, the Dusk-Mother | Death, memory, mourning | House **Mourne** took her name; the rites of the dead |
| **Korr**, the Deep-Smith | Earth, stone, making | The Forgekin claim descent; the first forges |
| **Sylvae**, the Green Mother | Life, growth, the wild | The Sidhe and the surviving Deep places |
| **Threne**, the Tide | Endings, entropy, the unmaking | The dread the cult of oblivion courts — and Sallow's creed |

Because Aurel was the keystone, **its murder broke the whole song** — the other
voices fell silent with it. The Choir is not a religion of worship so much as a
remembered fact the modern world has paved over.

## The timeline

- **The Mythic Age (Before Silence).** The Weave whole, the Choir singing; the
  high-fantasy golden age the Sidhe still mourn ([characters](characters.md): Maren).
- **The Discovery (≈ BS 5).** Proto-House alchemists learn that a dead old power can
  be *refined* into fuel — the first grist, from the first murdered wyrm. The secret
  that ends the world is an accounting insight.
- **The Sundering / The Silencing (AS 0).** The founding Houses murder the Choir to
  industrialize the discovery. Aurel falls and becomes the corpse-mountain; the
  Weave breaks; magic becomes scarce overnight. Publicly remembered as an
  impersonal cataclysm the Houses *survived* and led humanity out of.
- **The Founding Era (AS 0–100).** Vanta is built into Aurel's corpse; the grist
  economy takes hold; House **Quill** authors the salvation-myth everyone still
  believes ([factions](factions.md)).
- **The Binding Wars (AS 100–300).** The remaining great old powers are hunted and
  caged as the **Bound** (the world's reactors); the Sidhe are subjugated into an
  underclass.
- **The Long Refinement (AS 300–900).** Centuries of industrial expansion;
  strip-mining creates the **ashlands**; the Weave's fade quietly accelerates.
- **The Present (≈ AS 920).** The world is visibly running down — dimming color,
  failing harvests of ichor, rising grist scarcity. That scarcity is the pressure
  the plot turns on, and the problem **Mr. Sallow** proposes to "solve" for good
  ([story](story.md)).

## The hidden truth (writer's canon)

Layered exactly as the mystery reveals it ([story](story.md)): the Sundering was
deliberate deicide for profit; grist is refined souls and god-flesh; Aurel is not
dead but *dying slowly*, and the world's entropy is its death-rattle; Sable is its
last coherent fragment. Keep surface lore (what characters believe) and deep lore
(what's true) distinct in all writing — the gap between them is the game.

## Languages & naming conventions

Consistency guide for all names and text:

- **Old Aurric (the Choir-tongue)** — sacred, sung, vowel-rich; mostly lost, kept by
  the Sidhe. *Aurel, Sylvae, Morra.* Use for gods, the Bound, ancient places, and
  the requiem-rite's lyrics.
- **Reach Common** — the modern clipped vernacular; corporate and practical. Use for
  daily life and slang (*grist, rendering, the Marrow, a runner*).
- **Sidhe names** — luminous, flowing: *Maren, Aisling, Lyr.*
- **Forgekin names** — grounded, consonant, craft-flavored, often with a trade
  surname: *Tobiah Vesk, Garran Holt.*
- **Corporate / House names** — surnames-as-brands, cold and clean: *Mourne,
  Caldecott, Vesper, Quill;* the antagonist's *Sallow* (a color, a sickness, a
  ledger-grey).

## Culture & daily life

- **Grist is the water you swim in.** It is money, light, heat, medicine, and magic
  ([progression-and-economy](../design/progression-and-economy.md)). Everyone uses
  it; almost no one lets themselves think about where it comes from.
- **The open secret of rendering.** That people are rendered into black grist is
  known the way exploitation is always known — half-denied, joked about, feared.
  Crossing into the Marrow is crossing into where it's visible
  ([world](world.md)).
- **The Founding.** The Concord's great holiday celebrates the founders who "saved"
  humanity after the Sundering — i.e., the world unknowingly celebrates its own
  deicide. A perfect set-piece backdrop ([side-content](../design/side-content.md)).
- **The Sidhe diaspora.** A subjugated, fading people who remember the truth and are
  not believed; their requiem-rites secretly keep the Choir's song alive
  ([story](story.md)).
- **Augments & frames** — body modification is ordinary up-tier (fashion, status)
  and desperate down-tier (survival, debt); chrome on flesh is the era's signature
  ([world](world.md)).
- **Attitudes to magic** — the Craft is industrial and unromantic to most; the idea
  that magic was once *alive and free* sounds like a fairy tale. That disbelief is
  the tragedy ([themes-and-tone](themes-and-tone.md)).
