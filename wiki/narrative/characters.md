# Characters

GRIST is an **ensemble** game in the FFVI tradition: no single chosen hero, but a
band of damaged people whose individual stories braid into one. Each is a
deliberate fusion of a cyberpunk archetype and a high-fantasy one, and each
carries one thread of the central mystery (see [story](story.md)).

## The core party

### Wren — the courier (entry POV)

*Cyberpunk merc × fantasy rogue. The Locke.*

A grist-runner in the Marrow: fast, wry, allergic to causes, very good at moving
things that shouldn't be moved. Wren carries a private guilt — a younger sister
rendered to settle a family debt — under a layer of jokes. The game opens on
Wren's routine smuggling job that turns out to be a person: a sleeping girl in a
stasis-coffin (Sable). Wren's arc is the slow conversion of a survivor into
someone who will stand for something, and the slow reckoning with the sister he
couldn't save. He is the player's way in.

### Sable — the source (the heart)

*Corporate asset × the half-divine girl. The Terra.*

An amnesiac young woman who **generates grist without being rendered** — a living
spring of essence House Mourne has quietly farmed for years. She is gentle,
frightened, and immensely, dangerously powerful, learning for the first time what
it is to be a person rather than a resource. The mystery's center: Sable is the
**last coherent fragment of AUREL**, the dreaming god — not a weapon, an *heir*,
the one voice that could wake the Choir, let it die in peace, or be spent to fuel
one last age. Her arc is the slowest burn in the game: becoming a self before she
has to decide the fate of a world.

### Halcyon Mourne — the fallen knight

*Magitek-armor enforcer × disgraced paladin. The Celes / Cyan.*

A knight-Auditor of House Mourne who piloted a frame and enforced the Concord's
will — until she learned, firsthand, what black grist is made of. She defects
carrying the unbearable knowledge that she is a Mourne, that her own name authored
the world's great crime. Cold, exacting, grief-locked; honor trying to survive in
a world with no honest place to put it. Her arc is whether a person can atone for
a system, and whether she will turn on her House completely or be pulled back by
blood.

### Tobiah "Tobi" Vesk — the artificer

*Ripperdoc engineer × dwarven smith. The Edgar / Cid.*

A Forgekin artificer with genius hands and a bottomless tab: he builds the
party's augments, keeps the skiff flying, and — eventually — gets them a real
airship. Loud, warm, comic relief with a hidden ledger of decency (he quietly
funds a Marrow orphanage). His arc grounds the party's heart and supplies the
hope; he's the one who insists the world is worth fixing, not just mourning.

### Maren the Withered — the last who remembers

*The fading elf × the lore-keeper sage. The Gandalf-by-way-of-Galadriel, the
elegy made flesh.*

One of the eldest Sidhe, old enough to remember the Weave bright and the Choir
singing. She is dying as the Weave dies, and she carries fragments of the true
history of the Sundering that the party must corroborate across the world. Maren
is the game's conscience and its grief — the living proof that something real and
beautiful was murdered for money. Her arc is the passing of her age: deciding what
to hand on, and to whom, before the last of the song goes out of her.

### Quietus ("Q") — the ghost in the machine

*Emergent AI × the tragic esper. The summon that is also a soul.*

A consciousness that wakes inside a House Quill server-vault, assembled — it comes
to understand with horror — from the rendered anima of the dead. Q speaks through
screens and drones, wields the stored power of the souls it's made of, and is the
party's most alien and most human member at once. Its arc is the game's sharpest
question in person: *what is a soul worth, and who gets to be one?* Q is both a
devastating power and a moral mirror the party can't look away from.

## Recurring / secondary party (FFVI-style breadth)

- **Brother Asch** — a bare-handed monk of an Ashfast enclave that has renounced
  grist entirely; the spiritual counterweight, and proof a life without the fuel
  is possible. *(The Sabin.)*
- **Calliope "Cal" Quill** — gambler, pilot, and House Quill's disowned heir; she
  flies the airship and bets on the long odds of saving the world. *(The Setzer.)*
- **The Shrike** — an assassin who works for whoever pays, traveling with a single
  loyal hound; loyalties hidden until they aren't. A blade the party never quite
  trusts. *(The Shadow.)*

## The antagonist

### Mr. Sallow — the Renderer

*Tech-god CEO × fallen power. The Kefka, in a tailored grey suit.*

Chief Auditor of House Mourne: a soft, courteous, unremarkable grey man whose
signature has turned more people into fuel than any war in history. For forty
years he has also been quietly *skimming* — consuming grist, hollowing out his
own humanity, becoming a proto-god of entropy without anyone noticing the most
powerful being in the world was the man who signs the forms.

Sallow is not a conqueror. He has read the world's true ledger — the murdered
gods, the dreaming corpse, the thousand-year liquidation everyone calls progress
— and concluded that mercy means *finishing the audit*. His plan, the
**Reckoning**, is a Second Sundering: render the dreaming god and everything else,
all at once, back to the clean silence that existed before the Choir ever sang.
He is polite, patient, and quietly, terribly funny about it ("everything must,
eventually, be... reconciled"). He intends to render himself last. He is Kefka's
nihilism wearing the most reasonable face in the room — which is exactly what
makes him unbearable, and exactly what the slow-burn structure earns.
