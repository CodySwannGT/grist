# The world of GRIST

The known world is **Aurd**. Play takes place across its central supercontinent,
the **Mourning Reach** — colloquially, *the Reach*. It is a world in its long
dusk: the age of gods and magic is nine centuries dead, and what replaced it is
an industrial dystopia that runs, literally, on the remains.

## The Choir and the Weave (the mythic age)

In the beginning there was the **Choir** — the old gods, who did not *build* the
world so much as *sing* it. Their song was the **Weave**: the living magic that
ran through everything, the enchantment of the world. The brightest voice of the
Choir was **AUREL**, the Dawnfather, the World-Soul — the keystone of the song.

This was Aurd's high-fantasy age: the Weave bright and whole, the long-lived
peoples flourishing, the great old powers walking the world. It is the age the
oldest survivors still mourn. It is gone.

## The Sundering (the murder, ~900 years ago)

The public myth: a cataclysm, vast and impersonal, struck the world. The Choir
fell silent. The gods died, the Weave broke, and magic — once the air everyone
breathed — became scarce, hoarded, dangerous. From the ashes, the founders of
the great Houses dragged civilization back to its feet. They are remembered as
saviors.

The truth, buried for nine hundred years, is the engine of the whole story (see
[story](story.md)): the Sundering was **not an accident**. The founding Houses
**murdered the Choir** — methodically, for profit — because they had discovered
that a dead god could be *refined*. The Sundering was the first day of the grist
industry. Civilization is built on deicide, and almost no one alive knows it.

And AUREL did not entirely die. The greatest of the Choir is too large to die
quickly; it has been *dreaming its way into death* for nine hundred years. The
slow rot everyone calls entropy — the fading Weave, the dimming colors of the
world, the sense that everything is quietly running down — is the death-rattle of
a god no one remembers killing.

## Grist (what the world runs on)

When a god, or any old power, or eventually any living thing, dies under the
right conditions, it leaves behind **essence**. There are two raw forms:

- **Ichor** — divine essence. It still seeps, drop by drop, from AUREL's
  vast slow-dying corpse. The purest and most valuable source.
- **Anima** — the soul-residue of living things, rendered out at the moment of
  death.

Refined and crystallized, both become **grist**: the fuel, the currency, and the
magic of the modern world. Grist powers the lights, the frames, the skiffs, the
augments — everything. It is money. It is, increasingly, the only magic left.

There is a grade no one advertises. When demand outpaces the corpse and the
caught old powers, the Houses **render people** — debtors, undesirables, the
disappeared of the undercity — into **black grist**. The Marrow runs on the
dead, and most of its living don't let themselves know it.

> **The thesis in one word.** *Grist* is grain to be ground; *grist for the
> mill* is material to be consumed. In Aurd, the world, its people, and its gods
> are all grist. The name is the argument.

## Magitek, the modern way (the Craft)

The industrialized remains of the Weave are called **the Craft**. Where FFVI had
magitek, GRIST has a full grist-economy of it:

- **Frames** — grist-powered exo-armor; the magitek-knights of the corporate
  Houses. House enforcers pilot them. (See Halcyon in [characters](characters.md).)
- **Skiffs and dirigibles** — grist-driven airships, from courier skiffs to the
  great corporate barges. The party will, in time, come to own one.
- **Augments** — chrome-and-anima body modification; ripperdoc-artificers graft
  grist-tech into flesh. Cyberpunk's body-mod culture, fueled by refined souls.
- **The Bound** — the great old powers (wyrms and worse) the Houses captured
  rather than killed, caged as living reactors. Their suffering is a power source
  and an atrocity; freeing or wielding them is the game's "summon"/esper tragedy.

## The city of Vanta (the central hub)

AUREL's corpse fell at the heart of the Reach and became a mountain of dead god.
Over centuries the Houses grew a vertical megacity *into and over* it. **VANTA**
is the world's beating heart and the open world's central hub — explorable in
tiers, top to bottom:

- **The Crown** — corporate spires built on the god's skull and crown. The elite,
  the Houses, the boardrooms. Clean air, gold light.
- **The Tiers** — the middle city; the working world, markets, the Craft-shops.
- **The Marrow** — the undercity, inside the hollowed corpse. The poor, the
  underclass peoples, the rendering-houses. Neon and rain and ancient bone.
- **The Roots / the Deep** — the buried ruins of the kingdom that stood before
  the Sundering, far below, where wild Weave still pools and old things linger.

## The peoples of Aurd (high fantasy as underclass)

The fantasy races survived the Sundering into a world that no longer has a place
for them — the elegiac LOTR note, sharpened:

- **The Sidhe** ("the Withered") — the old enchanted folk; long-lived, beautiful,
  fading as the Weave fades. Once the keepers of the song, now a despised
  underclass who remember an age no human believes was real. (See Maren in
  [characters](characters.md).)
- **The Forgekin** ("Delvers") — the dwarven-descended; they built the first
  refineries and now staff them. Pragmatic, brilliant with their hands, the
  engineers and ripperdocs of the Marrow.
- **The Bound** — the dragons and great old powers, not a people anymore but
  prisoners; the world's caged batteries.

## The open world

Beyond Vanta, the Reach is the overworld: the **ashlands** (regions strip-mined
of grist, magic-dead and haunted), surviving **Deep places** (old forests and
ruins where the Weave still breathes), rival ruined city-states, and the wilds
where the Bound are caged. The map is a single living place in Act I — and the
*same* place, mourned and broken, after the midpoint catastrophe. See
[story](story.md) for how the world turns.
