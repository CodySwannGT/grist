# Factions & powers

Aurd's politics are **corporate-feudal**: the old kingdoms died in the Sundering
and the Houses that refined the gods inherited the world. They are corporations
with bloodlines, boardrooms with banners. See [world](world.md) for the setting
and [characters](characters.md) for the people who embody these powers.

## The Concord (the ruling cartel)

The four Great Houses rule Vanta and the Reach through **the Concord** — a cartel
dressed as a government. The Concord sets the price of grist, which means it sets
the price of everything: light, heat, magic, life.

### House Mourne — the Throne

The founding House and the master refiners. Mourne owns AUREL's corpse and the
flow of ichor; it is, quietly, the oldest and richest power in the world, and the
House with the most to hide about what really happened at the Sundering. Old
blood, old guilt, old money. Its enforcers pilot frames; its **Auditors** sign
the rendering orders. The antagonist, **Mr. Sallow**, is Mourne's Chief Auditor.
Halcyon was one of its knights.

### House Caldecott — the Anvil

Heavy industry and war: the frames, the skiffs, the great barges, the weapons.
Caldecott employs most of the Forgekin and most of the Marrow's labor. Brutal,
proud, the muscle of the Concord — and increasingly resentful of Mourne's grip on
the fuel that makes all its machines run.

### House Vesper — the Needle

Biotech and anima-work: augments, medicine, and the rendering trade itself.
Vesper turns flesh into chrome and the dead into grist; it is where the
undercity's black-market ripperdocs learned their craft and where the worst of
the rendering happens behind clean glass.

### House Quill — the Mirror

Media, finance, law, and surveillance. Quill owns the story everyone believes —
including the comfortable myth of the Sundering. It runs the screens, the
ledgers, the courts, and the vast server-vaults where information (and, it turns
out, *anima*) is stored. The waking machine-consciousness **Quietus** is born in
a Quill vault; the gambler **Calliope Quill** is the House's black sheep.

## The undercity & the resistance

- **The Marrow's own** — the underclass of Vanta: Sidhe, Forgekin, debtors, the
  disappeared. Not an organization so much as a vast, exploited population, and
  the moral center of the game. Wren is one of them.
- **The Unrendered** — a loose, fractured resistance movement: some idealists,
  some terrorists, some just people who refuse to be fuel. They know fragments of
  the truth about grist and disagree violently about what to do with it. A source
  of allies, betrayals, and moral grey throughout.
- **The Ashfast enclaves** — communities that have renounced grist entirely and
  live magic-poor but unrendered in the wilds. Brother Asch's order is one.

## The old powers

- **The Choir** — the dead gods. Silent for nine centuries. **AUREL**, the
  greatest, is not quite dead: it dreams beneath Vanta, and that dream is the
  hidden gravity the whole plot orbits.
- **The Sidhe remnant** — the last of the Withered who still carry pieces of the
  true history and the old song. Politically powerless, spiritually central.
  Maren is among the eldest.
- **The Bound** — the caged great old powers used as reactors. Whether they are
  monsters, prisoners, or the last free gods is one of the questions the party
  has to answer with their actions.

## The antagonist's position

**Mr. Sallow** is not opposed to the Concord — he *is* the Concord, its purest
distillation. He has spent forty years signing the orders that turn people into
fuel, and he has drawn the only honest conclusion available to a man in his
chair: the world is already a corpse being looted, and the merciful thing is to
finish the job cleanly rather than let the looting drag on for another thousand
years. His plan, the **Reckoning**, is the Concord's logic taken to its end —
total liquidation. See [characters](characters.md) and [story](story.md).
