# Open world

GRIST is open-world in the FFVI sense extended to a modern scale: a connected,
explorable Reach with a great central hub, where the **same map is lived in and
then mourned** across the two world-states ([story](../narrative/story.md)). This
page covers the map, traversal, gating, and how the world turns. Setting detail is
in [world](../narrative/world.md); side-content density is in
[side-content](side-content.md).

## Structure: a hub city + an overworld

### Vanta — the vertical hub

The megacity grown into the dead god's corpse ([world](../narrative/world.md)) is
the central, persistently-explorable hub. Its **tiers are the zones**, and
**descent is the basic gesture** — wealth and lies at the top, truth and the dead
at the bottom:

| Tier | Character | Reveals |
|---|---|---|
| **The Crown** | Corporate spires, gold light, the Houses | The comfortable myth |
| **The Tiers** | Working city, markets, Craft-shops, the hub's services | The machinery of daily complicity |
| **The Marrow** | Undercity, neon + rain + bone, the underclass, rendering-houses | What the fuel is made of |
| **The Roots / the Deep** | Buried pre-Sundering ruins, pooled wild Weave | The world before the murder |

### The Reach — the overworld

Beyond Vanta, a connected overworld of distinct regions: **ashlands** (strip-mined,
magic-dead, haunted), **Deep places** (old forests/ruins where the Weave still
breathes), **rival ruined city-states** (each its own faction flavor and side-arc),
and **the wilds** where the **Bound** are caged. Regions are hand-authored zones in
the FFVI/JRPG tradition — dense and legible, not procedural sprawl.

## Traversal — earned freedom

Mobility opens up the way it does in the classics, pacing exploration to the story:

1. **On foot / transit** — early Vanta and its immediate surroundings.
2. **The skiff** — a courier grist-skiff (Wren's world) opens regional travel.
3. **The airship** — Tobi builds / Cal flies the party's dirigible
   ([characters](../narrative/characters.md)) at a major beat, unlocking the full
   Reach. The classic "the world opens up" moment — and it lands differently when
   the world you've freed is already dying.
4. **Fast-travel** between discovered safehouses (a grist-cost service), so depth
   never becomes tedium.

## Gating: soft, not urgent ("no rush")

True to the slow-burn pillar ([overview](overview.md)):

- **Soft gates over hard timers.** Progress is gated by *capability and knowledge*
  (a traversal unlock, a mystery layer, a Bound freed) rather than by clocks. The
  fiction almost never rushes you.
- **The world rewards wandering.** Most lore, the best side-stories, and much of
  the mystery are *found*, not pushed (environmental storytelling; see
  [story](../narrative/story.md)).
- **Level/build gating is gentle.** Regions suggest a power band but rarely wall
  you out — going somewhere "too early" is a viable, dangerous choice.

## The Bound as open-world setpieces

The caged old powers ([combat](combat.md), [factions](../narrative/factions.md)) are
the open world's signature destinations — part dungeon, part boss, part moral
event. Each is a discoverable site where you choose to **free** or **wield** it.
They double as optional superbosses for the deep-grind audience and as the spine
of the magic-progression system ([progression-and-economy](progression-and-economy.md)).

## The world-turn: one map, two states

GRIST's defining structural move ([story](../narrative/story.md)):

- **Act I — The Reach (World of Balance).** The open world as a living dystopia:
  full Vanta, vivid (if rain-soaked) regions, the mystery building.
- **The Reckoning (midpoint).** Mr. Sallow's Second Sundering renders a swath of
  the Reach; lower Vanta and a region are liquidated; the party scatters.
- **Act II — Ashfall (World of Ruin).** The **same map, transformed**: broken
  Vanta, ash where regions stood, the Weave guttering, the desaturation motif at
  full strength ([themes-and-tone](../narrative/themes-and-tone.md)). More open and
  nonlinear — you reassemble whoever you can find
  ([side-content](side-content.md)) and decide a dying world's fate.

**Design rule:** every region, NPC arc, and landmark is authored to **read in both
states** — Ashfall is the elegy for places the player learned to love in Act I.
The same vista, mourned.

## Discovery & rewards

Exploration pays in grist, salvage, schematics, Bound shards, lore, and side-
stories. Curiosity is the most reliable income — fitting for a game where the
reward for looking closely at the world is learning what it's really made of.
