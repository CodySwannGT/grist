# Progression & economy

Character growth and the grist economy are **the same system** — which is the
whole point (see the pillars in [overview](overview.md)). You grow by spending the
substance the world is dying for. This page covers how characters build and how
grist flows. Combat use of these systems is in [combat](combat.md).

## The build identity: grist-augment

Locked in
[decisions/0002-core-game-design-pillars](../decisions/0002-core-game-design-pillars.md):
characters grow through two intertwined, freeform systems — fused FFVI espers and
cyberpunk chrome — while keeping a signature identity.

### 1. Bound shards (esper-style learned magic)

- Equip a **Bound shard** (won from a caged old power; see [combat](combat.md) and
  [open-world](open-world.md)). Equipped shards grant a **stat-growth bias** on
  level-up (the FFVI esper-bonus loop) and **teach spells over time** as the
  character earns Anima in battle.
- Spells, once learned, are **kept permanently** — so shards are how a character's
  Craft repertoire is *authored by the player*. Swap shards to steer who becomes
  what.
- Higher-grade **wielded** shards teach faster and stronger but accrue
  **corruption** (the Overdraw thread from [combat](combat.md)) — a build choice
  with a moral and mechanical tail.

### 2. Chrome augments (slotted passives & actives)

- Ripperdoc-artificers (Tobi; see [characters](../narrative/characters.md)) install
  **augments** into a character's slots: passive stat lines, resistances, utility,
  and **active** abilities (the *Augment* combat verb).
- Augments are **crafted and upgraded with grist**, salvaged, or bought. Slots are
  limited, so builds are about trade-offs, not stacking everything.
- Augments express the cyberpunk half of the fusion and let two players build the
  "same" character completely differently.

### Levels & signature kits

Characters still gain **levels** (HP/AP, base stats, shard-biased growth) and each
keeps a small **hand-authored signature** (Wren's runner mobility, Halcyon's
frame affinity, Sable's source-power, Q's borrowed-soul abilities) so identity
survives total build freedom.

## Grist: the one resource that is everything

Grist is **currency, crafting material, progression fuel, and the world's literal
fuel — all at once.** There is no separate "gold." When you spend money, you are
spending refined essence.

### Sources (how grist flows in)

| Source | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Combat salvage** | Defeated enemies/machines drop raw essence; clean kills yield more than Rendering-kills |
| **Contracts & bounties** | The Houses, the Unrendered, and locals pay in grist ([side-content](side-content.md)) |
| **Salvage & exploration** | Ruins, the Deep, ashland caches — exploration is paid in grist |
| **Refining** | Convert raw ichor/anima/salvage into refined grist at higher purity (a crafting step) |
| **Rendering** *(moral shortcut)* | The highest yield, from rendering the living/the Bound — fast power at a story cost the game remembers |

### Sinks (how grist flows out)

- **Augments** — craft, install, upgrade
- **Learning acceleration & shard refinement** — push the Bound systems
- **Gear & frames** — weapons, chrome, frame maintenance
- **Combat spend** — *Bind*, *Render* spells, revives ([combat](combat.md))
- **Services & the world** — fast-travel, safehouses, bribes, and reputation

The deliberate tension: **almost everything you want costs the same thing you'd
also burn to win a hard fight.** Scarcity is felt, and it's thematic.

## The grind, on purpose

GRIST is a *slow, grindy* game by design — but the grind is **opt-in depth**, not
a tax (see [overview](overview.md)):

- The **main path** is tuned to be beatable near 40 hours without heavy grinding.
- The **systems** (deep shard mastery, full augment builds, refining chains,
  freeing every Bound, completionist side-stories) reward players who *choose* to
  go deep, comfortably pushing well past 40 hours.
- The accumulative loop — earn grist, refine it, invest it, feel the slow climb —
  is the satisfaction the name promises. *Grist for the mill.*

## The moral economy (rendering)

The single most important economic choice recurs all game: **render for grist, or
not.** Rendering yields the most power, fastest — and it is the exact crime the
world is built on. Rendering choices feed **faction reputation** and **gate
endings** ([story](../narrative/story.md), [side-content](side-content.md)). The
game never blocks the shortcut; it just remembers. Your build is a record of what
you were willing to spend.

## Crafting & refining

A light, theme-true crafting layer: **refine** raw essence into grist grades,
**fabricate** augments and gear from grist + salvaged schematics, and **attune**
Bound shards. Tobi's workshop (and, later, the airship) is the hub. Recipes are
discovered through exploration and side-stories, not grinding menus.
